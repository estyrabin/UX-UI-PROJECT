Travel Stories & Inspirations

Port Grimaud, France

Often called the “Venice of Provence,” Port Grimaud immediately won me over with its pastel houses lining the canals. I loved how every home seemed to have a boat waiting at the doorstep, giving the whole place a relaxed yet elegant charm. Walking along the quays felt like stepping into a postcard, but without the overwhelming crowds of nearby Riviera towns.

My tip: Go early in the morning when the canals are quiet and the reflections in the water are at their best for photos. Renting a small boat gave me a whole new perspective of the colorful façades, and if you’re here on a Sunday, don’t miss the market—it’s full of local flavors and Provençal treasures.

Annapurna, Nepal

Trekking in the Annapurna region was one of the most humbling experiences of my travels. From the terraced fields to the rhododendron forests and the snow-capped peaks, every step felt like walking through a living painting. The warmth of the villages along the way made the journey even more special—I’ll never forget the smiles and the endless plates of comforting dhal bhat shared by locals.

My tip: Give yourself at least 10–12 days if you can; rushing through would mean missing the magic of watching the mountains change with the light. Start your hikes early before the clouds roll in, always carry some cash for the remote villages, and don’t underestimate the power of dhal bhat to keep you going.

Burano, Italy

Burano felt like stepping into a painter’s palette. Every canal was framed by houses in bold, joyful colors, and I loved learning how families have been passing down these colors for generations. Beyond the beauty, I also discovered the island’s lace-making tradition—it was inspiring to see artisans preserving this delicate craft.

My tip: Take the vaporetto early in the morning before the crowds arrive and wander into the quieter alleys—that’s where I found the most photogenic corners. If you get hungry, sit down for a risotto de gò, a fish dish unique to Burano that instantly became one of my Venetian favorites.
